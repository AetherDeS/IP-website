First pronunciation

The term meta-universe was first used by sci-fi writer Neal Stephenson in his novel Avalanche (1992). Nearly 20 years later, top executives at Microsoft, Epic Games and Meta are all talking about developing their own metaworld at the same time. Each company presented the virtual universe in its own way, but the fact remains. Corporations saw great potential in metaworlds and became interested in creating content for them.

Many people confuse the metaworld with abstract cyberspace.
1) Whereas in online services the user ties a real card, in the metaworld there is a currency.
2) In the metaworld, not only people communicate, but also learn and work. For example, with NextMeet service companies create a 3D office and hold meetings in it.
3) Brands and content creators will get more opportunities for product development and scaling.
4) In a perfect world, the content of the meta-universe depends only on communities and users. With the arrival of companies.

Blockchain
This is a distributed database that contains information about all transactions made by system participants. The information is stored as a chain of blocks. Each block contains a certain number of transactions. It is this technology that made cryptocurrency and NFT real. Thanks to blockchain today, content creators, artists, celebrities and other enthusiasts create virtual things and art objects for sale in the meta-universe.
Artificial Intelligence
Artificial Intelligence (AI) - is useful to people in very different professions: financiers and analysts use AI to predict risks and prevent financial fraud, AI helps forecasters make more accurate weather forecasts, doctors make diagnoses, and teachers check students' tests and essays. And projects that have only recently begun to emerge can already create works of art.

This kind of activity can also be dangerous, but predicting the consequences of creating artificial intelligence is almost pointless. Right now we are incredibly far away from the final goal, but we can assume some things. No one forbids us to speculate about the positive and negative consequences of creating AI.
Augmented reality
Augmented reality (AR) - is a technology that adds digital objects to the real, physical world. For example, a military pilot's helmet with glass displaying the distance to the target, flight altitude, different angles and a bunch of other combat parameters; a phone screen where the image of a pokémon is superimposed on the picture from the camera; a product package that turns into an exciting animation when the camera is pointed at it.
Internet of Things
The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of physical devices that are connected to and exchange data with other devices and services over the Internet or another network. There are currently more than a billion connected devices in the world, and more every year. Anything that can be equipped with sensors and software can be connected via the Internet.

IoT allows companies to automate processes and reduce labor costs. This reduces waste, improves services, and makes manufacturing and logistics cheaper.

The main problem associated with the development of IoT is security. Cybercriminals are constantly trying to hack into remote patient monitoring devices, databases with information about human health, intelligent vehicle control systems, commit phishing attacks, upload viruses to hacked devices, and even commit entire sabotage of production facilities. So IoT market participants need to learn how to protect their systems.

The Future of the Internet of Things IDC predicts there will be 55.7 billion connected devices worldwide by 2025. Cybercriminals will continue to attack them because the IoT system is a fast enough way to spread malware. Average users, businesses and entire cities will increasingly use smart technology to save time and money. For example, refrigerators will be able to warn of impending food spoilage, traffic lights with built-in video sensors will regulate traffic depending on the traffic.
